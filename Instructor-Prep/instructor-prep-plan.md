# FISH 290 — Instructor preparation plan

**Course:** Scientific Writing and Communication  
**Meetings:** Monday and Wednesday, 1:30–2:50pm  
**Source:** Syllabus-2026.docx  

---

## Standing weekly rhythm (every week)

**By Friday before the week**

- Confirm Canvas has PDFs, links, and due dates for the coming week (readings, templates, exemplars, peer-edit checklist).
- Skim student work from the prior check-in (if any) and flag 2–3 common issues to address Monday without naming individuals.
- Update the “living document” note on the schedule if you are shifting timing or emphasis.

**Sunday / Monday morning**

- Finalize Monday’s slides or prompts; print or queue any in-class handouts.
- Prep **discussion anchors** (1–2 concrete prompts tied to readings or a short example to mark up live).
- If there is a **check-in deadline** that week, set aside time to review submissions within 48 hours so feedback reaches students before the next milestone.

**Before Wednesday**

- Adjust Wednesday plan based on what Monday revealed (pace, confusion, missing data).
- Queue any **live demo** materials (library search, track-changes demo, figure critique).
- If it is a **heavy work** day (writing session, poster crunch), decide your circulation pattern: who you will visit first, what “done for today” looks like on the board.

**After each class**

- Post a short Canvas announcement: what happened + what is due next + any files to bring.
- Note names of students who may be stuck (data, topic scope) for a nudge before the next checkpoint.

## Week-by-week: what to prepare

### Week 1 (Mar 30, Apr 1)

- **Materials:** syllabus slides; ungraded writing prompt; link or PDF for “Science writing as storytelling” and “Reading scientific papers.”
- **Plan:** icebreakers that surface motivation and project anxiety; explicit model of how ideas become hypotheses next week.
- **Ops:** explain Google Drive / naming early if you use it for articles and poster PDFs later.

### Week 2 (Apr 6, Apr 8)

- **Mon:** Prep a concise **peer review** explainer (flow from submission to revision); bring 1–2 anonymized examples of reviewer tone (constructive vs. not).
- **Wed:** **Library session** — confirm liaison or your own demo path (research guides, databases); have a practice search topic ready if screenshare fails.
- **Between classes:** Be ready to help students tighten H₀/Hₐ language.

### Week 3 (Apr 13, Apr 15)

- **Mon:** Slides on paper structure and **Introduction**; plan compare/contrast of student poster papers (similarities/differences).
- **Wed:** **1-minute talks** — timer, order, norms (“what we’re listening for”).
- **Materials:** mock introductions file for track-changes; Orejas et al. intro for outline assignment.
- **Grading prep:** Rubric or quick checklist for tracked intro edits + outline (consistency with “5 pivotal paragraphs” lesson).

### Week 4 (Apr 20, Apr 22)

- **Mon:** Methods framework and **in-class data deadline** — be explicit and visible: “data in hand by end of class.” Plan troubleshooting corners (format issues, missing variables).
- **Wed:** **Writing studio** — your role is coaching and triage, not lecturing; bring a 1-page Methods “sanity checklist.”
- **Check-in (Sat):** Block time for **Intro + Methods** Canvas review; short feedback that points to next week’s results work.

### Week 5 (Apr 27, Apr 29)

- **Figures / tables** — gather 2–3 **good and bad** examples (crop from papers or make fake ones).
- **Check-in:** Define what “one figure or table with caption” must include (axis labels, sample size, clear takeaway).
- **Wed:** Leave room for **stats clarification** if many are stuck.

### Week 6 (May 4, May 6)

- **Mon:** Results prose + **mock results**; slide on interpreting null results.
- **Assignment:** “Photo of a campus poster” — you may want a submission folder or naming convention.
- **Wed:** Poster design basics (layout, hierarchy, **digital** display expectations).
- **Check-in (email):** Expect outline-level IMR + ≥1 figure/table — prepare a **template reply** for “you’re behind / you’re on track.”

### Week 7 (May 11, May 13)

- **Mon:** Discussion structure + limitations; keep energy for **poster due Wed**.
- **Wed — poster session:**
  - Rubric and note-taking sheet for audience feedback;
  - File deadline **12:00 PM** — confirm how you will collect/display (screen order, file types);
  - Plan to **forward anonymized comments** — set expectations for tone and turnaround.
- **Reminder:** **Sat May 9** paper progress (Intro/Methods/Results + figures/tables) is on syllabus — verify Canvas matches and that you will have bandwidth to review.

### Week 8 (May 18, May 20)

- **Mon:** Abstract architecture + in-class drafting.
- **Wed:** Work block; **complete first draft due** — specify “no bullet points, all sections”; decide whether you give global vs. inline feedback and by when.
- **Prep:** Peer-edit checklist ready for Week 9.

### Week 9 (May 25, May 27)

- **Mon:** Oral presentation principles; **peer-edit buddy pairs** (published in advance); in-class slide work.
- **Assignment:** First-draft peer edit using checklist.
- **Wed:** Paper **structure** integration; more presentation work time.
- **Check-in:** **Track-changes peer edits to you** — clarify file naming and whether you spot-check or read all.

### Week 10 (Jun 1, Jun 3)

- **Mon:** Brevity / cutting; in-class **paper + PowerPoint** polish.
- **Last day (Jun 3):**
  - **Final paper** 11:59 PM — Word, naming convention.
  - **Orals** — 10-minute hard stop; random order communicated early.
  - **Slides due 9:00 AM Monday** — syllabus says email PPT by 9:00 AM Monday before oral day; confirm whether that is **Monday Jun 1** and announce clearly if you use a different time.
- **Jun 9:** Optional overflow for talks — confirm room/booking and who must attend.

## High-leverage prep habits (whole quarter)

1. **Milestone map in one place** — May 9 progress, May 13 posters, May 20 full draft, Jun 3 final paper + talks, Jun 9 reserve — with Canvas shells and announcement dates locked.
2. **Parallel projects** — Every week, prep touches both **original project** (paper/talk) and **poster paper** so neither disappears.
3. **Participation** — Design each session so “attend + do activity = credit” is easy to verify without heavy grading.
4. **AI / integrity** — Revisit once when major writing begins (Week 4–5) and again before final draft.
