## Mon Apr 27 — Figures

### Teaching beats

- **Figure** when pattern, relationship, or distribution is clearer visually than in text  
- **Table** when exact values matter more than shape  
- **Neither** when a single number suffices in prose

### Bad figure bingo (discussion)

- Missing units, cropped axes that mislead, unreadable fonts, no sample size, rainbow colormap without need

## Wed Apr 29 — Tables & captions

### Caption formula (board)

**What** (panel/axis identity) + **How measured** (brief) + **N** + **Stats** (if shown) + **Takeaway** (what to notice).

---

## Email check-in — “One figure or table with caption”

### Minimum standard (publish to students)

**Figure:** axes labeled (quantity + unit), symbols/lines explained in caption or legend, **n** or sample identity clear, file resolution suitable for Word.  
**Table:** columns defined, units in header or footnote, **n** per group if relevant.  
**Caption:** can stand alone—reader does not need the main text to interpret the panel/table.

### Instructor reply templates

**On track:**  
> Thanks—this clearly shows [comparison/pattern]. Next: add [sample size in caption / axis unit / plain-language takeaway in last sentence].

**Needs revision:**  
> Core issue: a reader can’t tell [what is on y-axis / what n is / what points are]. Revise and resend by [date] or bring Wednesday.

## Stats triage FAQ (instructor)

- **“p-values only”** — Ask: what is the **effect size** or difference in means you will **describe** in Results?  
- **Wrong-test anxiety** — For class scope, prioritize **transparent** description over perfect model; note limitations in Discussion later.  
- **No replication** — Emphasize **single-study** language; avoid over-claiming.
