## Mon Apr 6 — Peer review & project refine

### Talking points: how a paper gets published

1. Author submits → **editor** desk review → **reviewers** invited  
2. Reviews return: major/minor, experiments, clarity  
3. **Revision** → decision (accept, reject, another round)

### Constructive vs. unhelpful review (examples)

Use **anonymized** or fictional snippets. Fill in your own before class:

| Constructive | Unhelpful |
|--------------|------------|
| “The prediction in line X conflicts with Fig. 2B; please clarify or reconcile.” | “The figures are bad.” |
| “Please cite recent work on [topic] to frame the gap.” | “This has been done before.” |

### Board prompts

- What is **reviewer #1** actually asking the author to **do**?
- How is peer review different from **grading**?

## Student handout — Hypotheses quick guide

**Null hypothesis (H₀):** Statement of “no effect” / “no difference” you can falsify with data (often the boring outcome).  
**Alternative hypothesis (Hₐ or H₁):** What you conclude if the data are inconsistent with H₀.

**Check:** Can your Hₐ be supported or refuted with the **dataset you can access**? If not, revise scope or data source.
