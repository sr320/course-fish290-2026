## Student handout — Five pivotal paragraphs (typical primary article)

Not every paper uses exactly five, but most strong introductions contain moves like:

1. **Context / importance** — Why should readers care about this system or problem?  
2. **Known** — What is established (with citations)?  
3. **Gap / unknown** — What is missing, contested, or unresolved?  
4. **Approach** — What did *this* study do (high level)?  
5. **Aim / objectives / hypotheses** — Precise endpoints the paper addresses.

**Task:** Label these moves in **your poster paper’s** Introduction. They may take more than one paragraph each—that’s fine.
